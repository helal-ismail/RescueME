Medical Background

 Introduction

 Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the number 1 cause of death globally. More people die annually from CVDs than from any other cause.
 An estimated 17.5 million people died from CVDs in 2012, representing 31% of all global deaths. Of these deaths, an estimated 7.4 million were due to coronary heart disease and 6.7 million were due to stroke.
 Out of the 16 million deaths under the age of 70 due to noncommunicable diseasesand 37% are caused by CVDs .
 People with cardiovascular disease or who are at high cardiovascular risk (due to the presence of one or more risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, hyperlipidaemia or already established disease) need early detection and management using counselling and medicines, as appropriate.
 Most of these deaths due to CVDs could be decreased if these patients found someone to recue them. So if we should deal with the fact that we have this huge number of CVD patients then we must find a way to save them before it's too late.

 About Rescue.ME

 Our medical team have been working on a set of patterns. Each pattern is a set of graphs of heart pulse and electrocardiograms limited with boundries.
 Once a patient's data falls into one of these patterns a patient is diagnosed with the condition the pattern represents.
 These patterns are verified against false alarms.
 A false alarm might be triggered by the cause of taking off the device which will result in no heart signal
 Another case might be practicing sport which may report a false Tachycardia.
 We are preventing those cases from sending a false alarm by not just considering the heart signal as a single parameter to depend on.
 But we also include the user's activity in the equation by measuring calories burnt and user activity like walking, running, etc. So the system will be fully aware of the user's current activity either he took the device off, resting or normally active.
 Our medical team consists of a staff of Senior and research assistant cardiologists.

Engineering Background

 Whether you join our platform with your own wearable device or you purchase the Rescue.ME band the process is all the same.
 First, a patient will need to pair his device with the Rescue.ME platform allowing our service to be able to access his medical data. After registeration, the patient will be asked to set his emergency contact list that can include any of his friends, relatives and doctors.
 Next, the device will be capturing and sending key bio metrics like heart rate, skin temperature and activity data in real time.
 Patient's data will be normalized, processed and analysed by our analytics platform. The platform compares the data against a set of patterns explained in the medical part.
 Once a patient's data falls inside one of these patterns, an alert is triggered. The platform will pull the patient's contact list and notify them via SMS, a recorded phone call, and even direct messaging on WhatsApp and similar services if available.
 To ensure scalability, real time operations and faster response, Our analytics platform operates over a standalone server and maintains the data in a MongoDB [ No SQL Style DB ] While the other basic operations is stored in a regular SQL DB.
 Our servers communicate together using a shared memory and an observer/observable pattern.
